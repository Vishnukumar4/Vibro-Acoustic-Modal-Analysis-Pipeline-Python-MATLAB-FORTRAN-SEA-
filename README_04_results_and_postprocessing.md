# README 04 — Results, Metrics, and Post-Processing

This file explains the engineering results generated by the vibro-acoustic simulation pipeline and how they are processed for reports or GitHub documentation.

---

## Main Result Metrics

| Metric | Meaning | Typical Plot |
|---|---|---|
| Modal density | Number of modes per frequency interval | Log-log vs frequency |
| Modal overlap | Degree of modal interaction | Log-log vs frequency |
| Radiation efficiency | Sound radiation effectiveness | Log or dB plot |
| Absorption coefficient | Fraction of absorbed acoustic energy | 0 to 1 vs frequency |
| Transmission loss | Sound insulation performance | dB vs frequency |
| Pressure RMS | Acoustic pressure response | Pa vs frequency |
| Velocity RMS | Structural vibration response | m/s vs frequency |
| Coupling loss factor | Energy transfer between subsystems | Log-log vs frequency |
| Insertion loss | Noise reduction after treatment | dB vs frequency |

---

## Transmission Loss

Transmission loss is one of the most important outputs in vibro-acoustic simulation.

```text
TL = -10 log10(tau)
```

where:

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `TL` | Transmission loss in dB |
| `tau` | Transmission coefficient |

Higher transmission loss means better sound insulation.

---

## Absorption Coefficient

Absorption coefficient describes how much incident acoustic energy is absorbed by a material.

```text
alpha = absorbed energy / incident energy
```

| Value | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0 | No absorption |
| 0.5 | 50 percent absorption |
| 1 | Perfect absorption |

Absorption plots are useful for evaluating porous layers, perforated plates, fibre materials, and noise-control treatments.

---

## Modal Density

Modal density describes how many modes exist per frequency interval.

Low modal density usually means deterministic methods such as FEM are more useful. High modal density usually means SEA becomes more appropriate.

```text
High modal density -> many overlapping modes -> SEA suitable
Low modal density  -> isolated modes -> FEM suitable
```

---

## Modal Overlap

Modal overlap shows whether modes are separated or strongly overlapping.

| Modal Overlap | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Low | Individual modes are clear |
| Medium | Transition region |
| High | Statistical modeling becomes useful |

This metric helps justify the use of hybrid FEM/SEA methods.

---

## Radiation Efficiency

Radiation efficiency describes how effectively structural vibration radiates sound.

A vibrating plate does not always radiate sound efficiently. Radiation behavior depends on frequency, panel size, bending waves, and coincidence frequency.

Important observations:

- Below coincidence, radiation may be inefficient.
- Near coincidence, radiation increases.
- At higher frequencies, radiation efficiency can become significant.

---

## Coupling Loss Factors

Coupling loss factors describe energy transfer between SEA subsystems.

Example:

```text
eta_12 = energy coupling from subsystem 1 to subsystem 2
eta_21 = energy coupling from subsystem 2 to subsystem 1
```

These values are important in SEA because they control power flow between rooms, plates, cavities, and junctions.

## Python Post-Processing Example

```python
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

freq = np.loadtxt("results/frequency_axis.csv")
tau = np.loadtxt("results/transmission_coefficient.csv")

TL = -10 * np.log10(tau)

plt.figure()
plt.semilogx(freq, TL)
plt.xlabel("Frequency [Hz]")
plt.ylabel("Transmission Loss [dB]")
plt.title("Double Wall Transmission Loss")
plt.grid(True, which="both")
plt.savefig("figures/double_wall_TL.png", dpi=300)
plt.show()
```

## Result Interpretation Notes

When writing the results section, explain not only what the plot shows but also why it happens.

Good explanation style:

```text
The transmission loss increases with frequency because the panel mass law behavior becomes stronger. The double-wall configuration shows additional improvement when the cavity is filled with fibre material, because the porous layer dissipates acoustic energy inside the cavity.
```

Avoid writing only:

```text
The plot shows transmission loss.
```
